Pesto Recipe

What makes Pesto, Pesto?

Pesto originates from Italy where people from hundreds of years ago made the sauce with a combination of basil, pinenuts, parmesan cheese, garlic, and olive oil. Now a days, people from all over the world have access to different recipes and alternatives to the basic ingredients.Today you will be learning my own recipe that my family and I have been making for over a decade. I am sharing our beloved recipe because I think everybody should experience the wonders of pesto.

The Ingredients

  • 2 cups of basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup of pine nuts
  • 1/3 cup of parmesan cheese
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1/3 cup of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  1. First, pick out good basil leaves from your basil container that you purchases. Then fill up 2 cups of fresh basil leaves into your food processor.
  2. Next, add a half cup of pine nuts with your basil leaves. This will add a fresh lasting taste in your pesto.
  3. After that you can add 1/3 of parmesan cheese and 4 cloves of garlic.Add you 1/3 cup of olive oil, 1 teaspoon of lemon juice, and 1/2 teaspoon salt and mix it all in the food processor for 10 second intervals.
  4. After the first interval you should taste test you flavors and see if you need anymore of any other ingredient. I normally find that I need a little more cheese on somtimes but on other days I need some more pine nuts.
  5. And lastly, don't panic if your pesto tastes a little overly salty. Matter of fact, it should taste a little salty because when you mix it with your pasta then the salt will get dilluted with your noodles and it won't be so overwhelming.
