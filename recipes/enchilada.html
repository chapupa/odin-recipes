Vegetarian Enchilada Recipe

The Beauty of Enchiladas

Enchiladas orignates from Mexico and has been dated back to Aztec civilizations. These delicious torilla contraptions make an excellent dinner for the holidays or just for a regular tuesday lunch. I will be showing you one of my mother's famous recipes and the one I like the most. You will learn the art of the enchilada and how to cook a delicious one.

The Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 diced onion
  • 1 pound of beyond meat crumbles
  • 1 can of beans
  • 15 small flour tortillas
  • 3 cups of cheddar cheese
  • 1 can of enchilada sauce
  1. First you want to preheat your oven to 350 degrees. While the oven is preheating you should take out a big/medium sized pan and a glass bakeware.
  2. After that you can start using the enchilada sauce to cover the bakeware and you can start heating up your pan.
  3. Once the pan is heated you can start putting the torillas on it to warm them up. You should be warming them on medium heat for about a minute on each side.
  4. When the tortillas been heated, you can lay it down flat on the bakeware and start putting sauce on it and your beyond meat, cheese, beans, and onions.
  5. You then roll it up into a little taco and put it face down where the two flaps are. You then cover it with more sauce until there isn't a part of the tortilla without sauce.
  6. You repeat this process with the other torillas until your backware is full. When it is all filled up, put whatever left over ingredients you have on top of all the rolled up tortillas
  7. Finally you place the backware in the oven at 350 degrees and let it bake for about 20 minutes. Serve and enjoy!
